(Correcting cloudiness)

See the difference,
before you decide.

A cataract is the clouding of the lens inside your eye. Early on it feels "as if you are looking through a dirty window." Drag the handle to see how surgery trades that haze for clarity.

← Cataract vision  ·  After surgery →

Cataract Clear

Signs you may notice

  • 01 Blurred or softened sharp images
  • 02 Dulled or faded light colors
  • 03 Increasing difficulty seeing at night
  • 04 Reading glasses or bifocals no longer help

Lowering your risk

  • 01 A diet rich in antioxidants (vitamin A, carotene)
  • 02 UV-protective glasses and lenses
  • 03 Quitting smoking (smokers face up to twice the risk)
  • 04 Regular exams to track changes early

The procedure, in about 15 minutes.

An outpatient procedure under microscope magnification. Once a cataract is removed, it cannot return.

01

Gentle anesthesia

Topical anesthesia without needles, plus light IV sedation for comfort.

02

Micro-incision

An incision of 2.8 mm or less allows ultrasonic phacoemulsification of the lens.

03

Lens removal

The cloudy lens material is removed while the clear lens capsule is preserved.

04

New lens implant

A foldable lens is inserted through the same incision and permanently replaces the old one.

Recovery is quick. Most patients feel minimal discomfort, wear a light protective shield, and return to normal activities within days. Vision improves soon after surgery and keeps getting better over several weeks.
